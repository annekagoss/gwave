Gravitational waves were detected for the first time on September 14th, 2015.
This is a visualization of that signal, named GW150914.


Gravitational waves are oscillations in spacetime curvature that originate from accelerating objects whose motion is not spherically or rotationally symmetrical. In this case, two mutually orbiting black holes with masses of 29 and 36 solar masses gradually lost gravitational energy. Their mutual orbital diameter decreased and they coalesced into a single black hole with a mass of 62 solar masses. The lost gravitational energy and remainding 3 solar masses were radiated in these gravitational waves.
As the resulting ripples propagated through spacetime, they expanded and contracted detectors on Earth by a factor of 10-21, or about 10 times the size of an atomic nucleus, but objects closer to the source were distorted much more.

The gravitational wave data was made public by the LIGO Scientific Collaboration (LSC) and the Virgo Collaboration. It signals mark the beginning of a new era of gravitational wave physics and provide previously undetectable information about the origins of the universe.
